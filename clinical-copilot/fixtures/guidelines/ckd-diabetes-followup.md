# CKD follow-up — demo guideline corpus (synthetic, no PHI)

## Monitoring creatinine

When serum creatinine rises above the patient's baseline, repeat the measurement within 1–2 weeks to confirm the trend before changing therapy. Document the collection date and compare against prior values.

## HbA1c targets in type 2 diabetes

For most non-pregnant adults with type 2 diabetes, target HbA1c below 7% if achievable without significant hypoglycemia. Values at or above 8% warrant intensification of lifestyle or pharmacologic therapy and reassessment of adherence.

## ACE inhibitor / ARB use

Patients with diabetes and elevated creatinine may remain on ACE inhibitors or ARBs if hyperkalemia is absent and creatinine rise is less than 30% from baseline. Review potassium and renal function within 1 week after dose changes.

## Patient-reported medications at intake

Verify intake-form medication lists against the active medication list in the chart at every follow-up. Discrepancies (e.g., lisinopril documented on intake but absent from e-prescribing) should be reconciled before visit close.

## Allergy documentation

Penicillin allergy documented on intake requires explicit avoidance of beta-lactam antibiotics unless allergy has been verified. Cross-reactivity with cephalosporins is low but clinically significant reactions should be treated as contraindications.

## Follow-up visit preparation

Summarize what changed since the last visit: new labs, intake updates, and guideline-relevant flags. Separate patient-record facts from guideline recommendations in the pre-visit brief.
